Our Research

Our Survey

Our field research shows that 90% of the adult population has at least one unused mobile phone in their household. Of these, 70% do not recycle because they don’t know how, or it’s too inconvenient to recycle.

For example, to recycle a phone with the City of Austin, you’d have to drive 20 miles each way to 290 and I-35 which is where the only city-run recycling service is available where you can recycle a phone.

Internet Research

  • Through literature review, we found the following remarkable facts.
  • E-waste is only 4% of the trash generated in the US, but it’s responsible for 70% of the chemicals released into the environment!
  • The E-waste produced in 2013 worldwide equalled 2.37 million tons. Only 12% of all e-waste is recycled.
  • A large part of what is labelled e-wasted is not waste, but usable older technology.
  • Many people burn e-waste which releases toxins like bromine and cadmium into the air and water.
  • For every 1 million cell phones that are recycled, 35,274 lbs of copper, 772 lbs of silver, 75 lbs of gold, and 33 lbs of palladium can be recovered.
  • Recycling 1 million laptops saves the energy equivalent to the electricity used by 3,657 U.S. homes in a year.
  • E-waste is still the fastest growing municipal waste stream in America, according to the EPA.
  • It takes 530 lbs of fossil fuel, 48 lbs of chemicals, and 1.5 tons of water to manufacture one computer and monitor.

Worldwide 235.6 million cell phones are manufactured in 2014 of which only 17.4 million cell phones are recycled. As of April 2014, there were 327,577,529 cell phones in the U.S.

  • The cadmium from one mobile phone battery is enough to pollute 600 m3 of water
  • Americans discard phones containing over $60 million in gold/silver every year and they do so frequently as new models are made available.
